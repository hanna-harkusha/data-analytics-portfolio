# 💰 Personal Finance & Habit Tracker (Google Sheets)

### 📌 Objective
To track, analyze, and visualize personal financial activity and daily habits throughout the year. The goal was to monitor income and expenses by category, identify spending patterns, evaluate monthly savings dynamics, and track daily mood and exercise habits for behavioral insights.

### 🧰 Tools & Methods
- **Google Sheets** – data organization and calculation  
- **Formulas & Functions** – automated totals, category summaries, mood averages, and habit counts using formulas (e.g., SUM, AVERAGE, COUNTIF, MODE, IFERROR)
- **Charts** – visualization of income, expenses, savings trends, mood progression, and exercise consistency  
- **Dashboard Sheet** – consolidated yearly overview of finances and habits  
- **Conditional Formatting** – color-coded mood and exercise markers for quick visual reference  

### 📋 Data Description
- **Finance Data**
  - Source: Manually entered personal finance records  
  - Structure: 12 monthly sheets + 1 total summary sheet  
  - Columns: Date, Category, Income, Expense, Balance  
  - Period: Full year (12 months)  
- **Habit & Mood Data**
  - Source: Daily self-tracking  
  - Columns: Date, Mood (%), Exercise (1-7), Notes  
  - Period: Full year (12 months)  
  - Mood recorded as percentage; exercise marked and visually highlighted with conditional formatting

### 📈 Results
- Clear visibility of income and expense distribution by month and category  
- Automated calculation of total savings and spending ratio  
- Identified high-expense categories and seasonal trends  
- Tracked mood fluctuations and exercise consistency with visual cues  
- Enhanced personal budgeting and habit awareness through integrated analytics
